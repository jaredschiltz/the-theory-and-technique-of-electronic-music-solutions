Chapter 1

1) A sinusoid has initial phase ϕ=0 and angular frequency ω=π10. What is its period in samples? What is the phase at sample number n=10 ?

f=1t = Rn=ωR2π⇒n=2πω

n=2πω=2ππ10=period is 20 samples

phase φ=ωn + ϕ

φ=πn10 + 0 = π1010=phase is π radians

2) Two sinusoids have periods of 20 and 30 samples, respectively. What is the period of the sum of the two?

Resultant period is the least common multiple of the two periods:

LCM(20, 30) = 60 samples

3) If 0 dB corresponds to an amplitude of 1, how many dB corresponds to amplitudes of 1.5, 2, 3, and 5?

ddB=20 log10(a)

a1.5= 3.52 dBa2= 6.02 dBa3= 9.54 dBa5= 13.98 dB

4) Two uncorrelated signals of RMS amplitude 3 and 4 are added together. What is the RMS amplitude of the sum?

Whenever COVxn , yn = 0,ARMS2 = ARMSxn2+ARMSyn2

ARMS2 = 32+42ARMS = 5

5) How many uncorrelated signals, all of equal amplitude, would your have to add to get a signal that is 9 dB greater in amplitude?

a = 10(9 dB20) = 2.818

A2 = A12+A22+A32+⋯ A2A = 2.82· a(2.82·a)2 = N(a)2

N = 7.95, so a total of 8 uncorrelated signals are required

7) Two sinusoids play at middle C (MIDI 60) and the neighboring C# (MIDI 61). What is the difference, in Hertz, between their frequencies?

fC=440·2(60-69)/12=261.626 HzfC#=440·2(61-69)/12=277.182 Hz∆f = fC-fC#=14.56 Hz

8) How many cents is the interval between the seventh and eight harmonic of a periodic signal?

2h12=8 ⇒ h = 36 half steps to 8th harmonic2h12=7 ⇒ h = 33.69 half steps to 7th harmonic∆h = 36 - 33.69 = 2.31 half steps, or 231 cents

9) If an audio signal x[n], n = 0, ... , N - 1 has peak amplitude 1, what is the minimum possible RMS amplitude? What is the maximum possible?

Minimum RMS amplitude is where x[n] = 1 for one sample, and all other samples zero:

ARMS_MIN = x[n]2N = 12N=1N =1N

Maximum RMS amplitude is where x[n] = 1 for all samples:

ARMS_MAX = N·x[n]2N = N·12N=1 = 1
